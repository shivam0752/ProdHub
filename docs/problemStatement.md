# ProdHub — Problem Statement

## What We're Building
ProdHub is a free, curated resource hub for people in India breaking into product management or early in their PM career. It centralizes frameworks, resources, and community knowledge filtered through the lens of someone going through the exact same transition — not packaged by a senior PM, not monetised, not SEO-optimised.

Inspired by the FMHY model: obsessive curation, no sponsored content, editorial judgment over volume.

## Who Built This and Why
Built by an aspiring PM. Not an authority — someone figuring it out in real time, sharing only what has actually helped, and being transparent about what is still unclear. That honesty is the product's credibility.

## Who It's For
**Primary:** Aspiring PMs in India — people from engineering, MBA, consulting, or BA backgrounds actively trying to break into product.

**Secondary:** Early-career PMs and APMs (0–3 years) at Indian product companies who need quick on-the-job reference material.

Not for: Senior PMs, CPOs, or anyone looking for project management tools.

## The Problem — Validated by 17 User Responses
A survey of 23 NextLeap fellows (May 2026) surfaced four real gaps:

1. **"I don't know if I'm actually thinking like a PM"** — 6 respondents independently said they know the frameworks but have no way to validate whether their thinking holds up. The gap between knowing RICE and knowing if their RICE score is right.

2. **"Which AI tools should I learn as a PM and how do I keep up"** — 3 respondents. Content gap, not tool gap. No curated, opinionated guide exists for PM-specific AI tools.

3. **"I can't connect user feedback to business metrics"** — 3 respondents. KPI trees, numerical targets, translating qualitative signals into justifiable decisions.

4. **"I don't know what a PM actually does day to day"** — 2 respondents. Absolute beginners who feel lost even after starting structured courses.

**The meta-problem:** Almost all PM advice online is US-centric. FAANG case studies, Silicon Valley benchmarks, YC-flavored thinking that does not map to Flipkart, Swiggy, Zepto, Razorpay, or CRED interview processes. There is no single trustworthy destination built specifically for the Indian PM transition.

## What Makes the Curation Trustworthy
- Every resource is something the curator personally found useful
- Every approved community submission includes one editorial sentence explaining why it belongs
- Nothing is here because it is popular — things are here because they are good
- No affiliate links, no sponsored placements, no SEO padding

## Success Metrics
- **Month 1:** 100 registered users, avg session depth >3 tools, 10+ community submissions
- **Month 3:** 500 registered users, 30+ approved resources with editorial notes, avg rating ≥4/5, organic shares in Indian PM communities on LinkedIn and WhatsApp
- **Month 6:** Referenced in Reddit threads and LinkedIn comments without prompting — becomes the default link people share when someone asks "where do I start for PM prep in India"

## What We Are Not Building
- A job board
- A course platform
- A resume builder or ATS scorer
- A community forum or chat
- A global PM aggregator that tries to cover everything
- A unique AI feature at MVP — ship clean, observe behaviour, let the feature reveal itself from real usage
